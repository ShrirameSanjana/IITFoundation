## BINOMIAL THEOREM
(i) The expansion of a binomial for any positive integral power $n$ is given by Binomial theorem, which is
$(a+b)^{n}={ }^{n} C_{0} a^{n}+{ }^{n} C_{1} a^{n-1} b+{ }^{n} C_{2} a^{n-2} b^{2}+\ldots \ldots \ldots . .+{ }^{n} C_{n-1} a . b^{n-1}+{ }^{n} C_{n} b^{n} ;$
where ${ }^{n} C_{r}=\frac{n!}{(n-r)!r!}, n!=n(n-1)(n-2)(n-3) \ldots \ldots 2.1 ; 0!=1!=1$ and $n$ is a non-negative integer.

$$
$$
\left[\because{ }^{n} C_{r}={ }^{n} C_{n-r}\right]
