## SOLUTION OF A PAIR OF LINEAR EQUATIONS IN TWO VARIABLES
To find two numbers such that their sum is 35 and their difference is 5 . We can represent the problem algebraically by letting $x$ represents the large number and $y$ represents the smaller number. The problem is indicated by the two equations as given below:
$$
\begin{aligned}
& x+y=33 \\
& x-y=5
\end{aligned}
$$
The two equations for which we seek a common solution are called simultaneous equations. The two equations, taken together, comprise a system of equations. The value of the variables $x$ and $y$ that satisfied the above system of equations is called its solution. Each of the above equations represents a straight line.
Graphically solution of a system of a pair of linear equations in two variables is the coordinates of the point where the two lines intersect each other.
## CONSISTENT, DEPENDENT AND INCONSISTENT SYSTEM OF EQUATIONS
System of a pair of linear equations in two variables :
$$
\begin{aligned}
& a_{1} x+b_{1} y+c_{1}=0 \\
& a_{2} x+b_{2} y+c_{2}=0
\end{aligned}
$$
The given system of a pair of linear equations in two variables has either one solution, infinite solutions or no solution. Note that the variables in both the equations are same.
Case (i); If $\frac{a_{1}}{a_{2}} \neq \frac{b_{1}}{b_{2}}$, then the system has only one (or unique) solution, and the system is called consistent.
In this case, a pair of straight lines represented by the system of equations intersect each other exactly at one point.
Example: Consider the system of linear equations $2 x-3 y=6$ and $x+y=1$
Here, $a_{1}=2, a_{2}=1, b_{1}=-3, b_{2}=1$
Now, $\frac{2}{1} \neq \frac{-3}{1} \Rightarrow \frac{a_{1}}{a_{2}} \neq \frac{b_{1}}{b_{2}}$
Intersecting lines
So, the system has unique solution.
Case (ii); If $\frac{a_{1}}{a_{2}}=\frac{b_{1}}{b_{2}}=\frac{c_{1}}{c_{2}}$, then the given system has infinite many solutions and the system is called dependent.
In this case, a pair of lines represented by the system of equations, coincides with each other.
So they intersect each other at infinite number of points.
Example: Consider the system of equations $3 x-y+5=0$ and $6 x-2 y+10=0$
Here, $a_{1}=3, a_{2}=6, b_{1}=-1, b_{2}=-2, c_{1}=5, c_{2}=10$
Now, $\frac{3}{6}=\frac{-1}{-2}=\frac{5}{10} \Rightarrow \frac{a_{1}}{a_{2}}=\frac{b_{1}}{b_{2}}=\frac{c_{1}}{c_{2}}$
Now, the system has infinite solutions.
Case (iii); If $\frac{a_{1}}{a_{2}}=\frac{b_{1}}{b_{2}} \neq \frac{c_{1}}{c_{2}}$, then the given system has no solution and the system is called inconsistent. In this case, a pair of lines represented by the system are parallel to each other.
So they do not intersect each other at any point.
Example: Consider the system of equations $x-5 y-6=0$ and $2 x-10 y-14=0$
Here, $a_{1}=1, a_{2}=2, b_{1}=-5, b_{2}=-10, c_{1}=-6, c_{2}=-14$
Now, $\frac{1}{2}=\frac{-5}{-10} \neq \frac{-6}{-14} \Rightarrow \frac{a_{1}}{a_{2}}=\frac{b_{1}}{b_{2}} \neq \frac{c_{1}}{c_{2}}$
Hence, the system has no solution.

## ALGEBRAIC METHODS OF SOLVING A PAIR OF CONSISTENT LINEAR EQUATIONS
A system of a pair of linear equations can be solved algebraically by following three methods.
(A) Substitution Method
(B) Elimination Method
(C) Cross-multiplication Method
## (A) First Method - Substitution Method
In substitution method, you have to express one of the variables in terms of the other using either of the equations and substitute this in other equation.
## Algorithm to find the solution :
A system of a pair of linear equations in two variables:
$$
\begin{aligned}
& a_{1} x+b_{1} y+c_{1}=0 \\
& a_{2} x+b_{2} y+c_{2}=0
\end{aligned}
$$
Step 1 : Find the value of one variable, say $y$ in terms of the other variable, i.e., $x$ from either equation, whichever is convenient. Consider this equation as equation (iii).
Step 2 : Substitute this value of $y$ getting from equation (iii) in either equation (i) or (ii). This gives an equation in one variable $x$, from which you can easily get the value of $x$.
Step 3 : Substitute the value of $x$ obtained in Step 2 in the equations (iii), and obtain the value of the other variable $y$.
## ILLUSTRATION : 3
## Find the solution of the following system by substitution method :
$$
4 x+y=11 ; x+2 y=8
$$
## SOLUTION :
$$
\begin{aligned}
& 4 x+y=11 \\
& x+2 y=8
\end{aligned}
$$
It is easy to find the value of $y$ in terms of $x$ from the equation or to find the value of $x$ in terms of $y$ from the second equation. Let us find the value of $y$ in terms of $x$ from the first equation. The result is $y=11-4 x$
Now putting the value of $y$ in equation (ii), we get
$$
x+2(11-4 x)=8
$$
We now have one equation that is linear in $x$; i.e the equation contains only the variable $x$.
On solving, we find that
$$
x+22-8 x=8 \quad \text { or } \quad-7 x=8-22 \quad \text { or } \quad-7 x=-14 \quad \text { or } \quad x=2
$$
To get the corresponding value of $y$, we substitute $x=2$ in equation (iii). The result is
$$
y=11-4(2)=11-8=3
$$
Thus, the solution of the given pair of linear equations are $x=2$ and $y=3$.
## (B) Second Method - Elimination Method
## Algorithm to find the solution:
Step 1 : If coefficients of one variable (either $x$ or $y$ ) in both the equations are equal, then go to step- 2 otherwise multiply one or both the equation(s) by some suitable non-zero constants to make the coefficients of one variable (either $x$ or $y$ ) numerically equal.
Step 2 : Add one equation to other or subtract one equation from the other so that one variable gets eliminated. In this case, you get an equation in one variable. By solving this equation you can easily get the value of the variable involved in this equation.
Step 3 : Substitute the value of the variable ( $x$ or $y$ ) obtained in step- 2 in either of the original equations to get the value of the other variable.
The elimination method is preferred when the numerical coefficients of one variable in the original equations are same.
## ILLUSTRATIOM : 4
The ratio of incomes of two persons is 9:7 and the ratio of their expenditures is $4: 3$. If each of them manages to save ₹ $\mathbf{2 0 0 0}$ per month, find their monthly incomes.
## SOLUTION :
Let the income of the two person be $₹ 9 x$ and $₹ 7 x$ and their expenditures be $₹ 4 y$ and $₹ 3 y$ respectively. Then the equations formed for the given situation are given by :
$$
\text { and } \quad \begin{aligned}
& 9 x-4 y=2000 \\
& 7 x-3 y=2000
\end{aligned}
$$
Step 1 : Multiply equation (i) by 3 and Equation (ii) by 4 to make the coefficients of $y$ equal. Then, we get the equations:
$$
\begin{aligned}
& 27 x-12 y=6000 \\
& 28 x-12 y=8000
\end{aligned}
$$
Step 2 : Subtract equation (iii) from equation (iv) to eliminate $y$, because the coefficients of $y$ are the same in equation (iii) and (iv). So, we get
$$
\begin{aligned}
& (28 x-27 x)-(12 y-12 y)=8000-6000 \\
\Rightarrow \quad & x=2000
\end{aligned}
$$
Step 3 : Substituting this value of $x$ in (i), we get
$$
9(2000)-4 y=2000 \quad \text { i.e., } \quad y=4000
$$
So, the solution of the equations: $x=2000, y=4000$. Therefore, the monthly incomes of the two persons are ₹ 18,000 and ₹ 14,000 , respectively.
Verification : $18000: 14000=9: 7$.
Also, the ratio of their expenditures $=18000-2000: 14000-2000=16000: 12000=4: 3$
## (C) Third Method - Cross-multiplication Method
## Algorithm to find the solution:
Step 1: Write the given equations in the form :
$$
\begin{aligned}
& a_{1} x+b_{1} y+c_{1}=0 \\
& a_{2} x+b_{2} y+c_{2}=0
\end{aligned}
$$
Step 2 : Taking the help of the diagram given below :
The arrows between the two numbers indicate that they are to be multiplied and the second product is to be subtracted from the first product.
Write equation as
$$
\frac{x}{b_{1} c_{2}-b_{2} c_{1}}=\frac{y}{c_{1} a_{2}-c_{2} a_{1}}=\frac{1}{a_{1} b_{2}-a_{2} b_{1}}
$$
Step 3 : If $a_{1} b_{2}-a_{2} b_{1} \neq 0$, then solve $\frac{x}{b_{1} c_{2}-b_{2} c_{1}}=\frac{1}{a_{1} b_{2}-a_{2} b_{1}}$ for $x$ and solve $\frac{y}{c_{1} a_{2}-c_{2} a_{1}}=\frac{1}{a_{1} b_{2}-a_{2} b_{1}}$ for $y$.

## SOLUTION OF A SYSTEM OF A PAIR OF EQUATIONS REDUCIBLE TO THE SYSTEM OF A PAIR OF LINEAR EQUATIONS IN TWO VARIABLES
By using the suitable substitution or simplification first we convert the given system into the system of a pair of linear equations in two variables. Then after using any algebraic or graphical method we solve the system.
## ILLUSTRATIOM : $\mathbf{6}$
Solve the equations : $\frac{2 x+1}{3}+\frac{3 y+2}{5}=2$ and $\frac{2(2 x+1)}{3}-\frac{3(3 y+2)}{5}=-1$
## SOLUTION :
Given equations are : $\frac{2 x+1}{3}+\frac{3 y+2}{5}=2$
...(i) and $\frac{2(2 x+1)}{3}-\frac{3(3 y+2)}{5}=-1$
Let $\frac{2 x+1}{3}=u$ and $\frac{3 y+2}{5}=v$
Then the equations become
$$
u+v=2 \quad \ldots \text { (iii) } \quad 2 u-3 v=-1
$$
Multiplying (iii) by 3, $3 u+3 v=6$.
Adding (iv) and (v), $5 u=5 \Rightarrow u=1$
Substituting this value of $u$ in (iii), $1+v=2 \Rightarrow v=2-1=1$
Then $\frac{2 x+1}{3}=u=1$ and $\frac{3 y+2}{5}=v=1 \Rightarrow x=1 \quad$ and $y=1$
## SOLVING THE WORD PROBLEMS
Many problems can be solved quickly and easily by converting them into a system of a pair of linear equations in two variables as follows :
(i) Represent the unknown quantities by any two variables say $x$ and $y$, which are to be determined.
(ii) Find the conditions given in the problem and translate the verbal conditions into a pair of simultaneous linear equations using the variables $x$ and $y$.
(iii) Solve these equations and obtain the required quantities with appropriate units.
