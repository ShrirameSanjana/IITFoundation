## Complex Numbers
If a and b are two real numbers, then a number of the form $\mathrm{z}=\mathrm{a}+\mathrm{ib}$ such that $\mathrm{i}^{2}=-1$ or $\mathrm{i}=\sqrt{-1}$ is called a complex number. Here $a$ is the real part denoted by $\operatorname{Re}(\mathrm{z})$ \& b is the imaginary part denoted by $\operatorname{Im}(\mathrm{z})$.
Suppose $\mathrm{z}_{1}=a+\mathrm{i} b \& \mathrm{z}_{2}=c+\mathrm{i} d$, here $\mathrm{z}_{1}=\mathrm{z}_{2}$
## Algebra of Complex Numbers
I. Addition : Let $\mathrm{z}_{1}=a+\mathrm{i} b, \mathrm{z}_{2}=c+\mathrm{i} d$, then $\mathrm{z}_{1}+\mathrm{z}_{2}=(a+c)+\mathrm{i}(b+d)$
II. Difference : $\mathrm{z}_{1}-\mathrm{z}_{2}=(a-c)+\mathrm{i}(b-d)$
III. Multiplication : Let $\mathrm{z}_{1}=\mathrm{a}+\mathrm{i} b, \mathrm{z}_{2}=\mathrm{c}+\mathrm{i} d$, then $\mathrm{z}_{1} \mathrm{z}_{2}=(a c-b d)+\mathrm{i}(a d+b c)$
## COMPLEX NUMBER
A number of the form $a+i b$, where $a, b$ are real numbers is called a complex number, where $i=\sqrt{-1}, a$ is the real part and $b$ is the imaginary part of the complex number. E.g : $7+2 i, 0+2 i, 1+0 i$, etc.
Note : (i) $i$ is read as iota. (ii) A complex number is usually represented by $z$. i.e., $z=a+i b$
(iii) Real part of $z$ is denoted by " $\operatorname{Re}(z)$ " and imaginary part of $z$ is denoted by " $\operatorname{Im}(z)$ "
## INTEGRAL POWERS OF IOTA
We have, $i=\sqrt{-1}$ and $i^{2}=-1$. So $i^{3}=i^{2} . i=(-1) i=-i \quad$ and $\quad i^{4}=\left(i^{2}\right)^{2}=(-1)^{2}=1$.
$\therefore \quad i^{4 n}=1$, where $n \in N$
The values of the negative integral powers of $i$ are found as given below :
$i^{-1}=\frac{1}{i}=\frac{i^{3}}{i^{4}}=i^{3}=-i, \quad i^{-2}=\frac{1}{i^{2}}=\frac{1}{-1}=-1, \quad i^{-3}=\frac{1}{i^{3}}=\frac{i}{i^{4}}=\frac{i}{1}=i, \quad i^{-4}=\frac{1}{i^{4}}=\frac{1}{1}=1$
Remarks : A complex number $z=a+i b$ is said to be
(i) Purely real, if imaginary part of $z=0$
(ii) Purely imaginary, if real part of $z=0$
## ILLUSTRATION : 1
Express the complex number $\left(\frac{1}{3}+3 i\right)^{3}$ in the form of $a+i b$.
## SOLUTION :
$$
\begin{aligned}
& \left(\frac{1}{3}+3 i\right)^{3}=\left(\frac{1}{3}\right)^{3}+3\left(\frac{1}{3}\right)^{2}(3 i)+3 \cdot\left(\frac{1}{3}\right)(3 i)^{2}+(3 i)^{3} \\
& =\frac{1}{27}+i+9(-1)+27 i^{3}=\frac{1}{27}+i-9+27 i \cdot\left(i^{2}\right) \\
& =-\frac{242}{27}+i+27 i \cdot(-1)=-\frac{242}{27}-26 i
\end{aligned}
$$
It is in the form of $a+i b$, on comparing, we get $a=-\frac{242}{27}, b=-26$
## CONJUGATE OF A COMPLEX NUMBER
The conjugate of a complex number is also a complex whose real and imaginary part are same but sign of imaginary part are opposite.
If $z=a+i b$ be a complex number, then its conjugate denoted by $\bar{z}$ is defined as $\bar{z}=a-i b$
## Properties of Conjugate of Complex Number
(i) $\overline{(\bar{z})}=z$
(ii) $\overline{z_{1} \pm z_{2}}=\overline{z_{1}} \pm \overline{z_{2}}$
(iii) $\overline{z_{1} \cdot z_{2}}=\overline{z_{1}} \cdot \overline{z_{2}}$
(iv) $\overline{\left(\frac{z_{1}}{z_{2}}\right)}=\frac{\bar{z}_{1}}{\bar{z}_{2}}$ if $\bar{z} \neq 0$
## ALGEBRAIC OPERATIONS WITH COMPLEX NUMBER
For any complex numbers, $z_{1}=a+i b$ and $z_{2}=c+i d$, we have
(a) Addition : $(a+i b)+(c+i d)=(a+c)+i(b+d)$
(b) Subtraction : $(a+i b)-(c+i d)=(a-c)+i(b-d)$
(c) Multiplication : $(a+i b)(c+i d)=(a c-b d)+i(a d+b c)$
(d) Division : $\frac{a+i b}{c+i d}$ ( when at least one of $c$ and $d$ is non-zero) $=\frac{(a c+b d)}{c^{2}+d^{2}}+\frac{i(b c-a d)}{c^{2}+d^{2}}$
## MODULUS OF A COMPLEX NUMBER $z=x+i y$
The modulus of a complex number $z=x+i y$ is denoted by $|z|$ and read as $\bmod (\mathrm{z})$. It is defined as the distance of point $(x, y)$ from origin in $X O Y$-plane (called Argand plane)
i.e., $|z|=\sqrt{x^{2}+y^{2}}$, where $x=\operatorname{Re}(z), y=\operatorname{Im}(z)$.
## ILLUSTRATIOM : 2
Find the modulus of the complex number $z=-1+i \sqrt{3}$
## SOLUTION :
$z=-1+i \sqrt{3} \Rightarrow|z|=\sqrt{(-1)^{2}+(\sqrt{3})^{2}}=\sqrt{4}=2$
## Properties of Modulus of Complex Number
(i) $|z|=0 \Rightarrow z=0$
(ii) $\quad z \cdot \bar{z}=|z|^{2}$
(iii) $\left|z_{1} z_{2}\right|=\left|z_{1}\right| \cdot\left|z_{2}\right|$
(iv) $\quad\left|\frac{z_{1}}{z_{2}}\right|=\frac{\left|z_{1}\right|}{\left|z_{2}\right|}\left(z_{2} \neq 0\right)$
(v) $|z|=|\bar{z}|$
(vi) $\quad|-z|=|z|$
(vii) $\left|z_{1}-z_{2}\right| \geq\left|z_{1}\right|-\left|z_{2}\right|$
(viii) $\quad\left|z_{1}+z_{2}\right| \leq\left|z_{1}\right|+\left|z_{2}\right|$
## MULTIPLICATIVE INVERSE OF A COMPLEX NUMBER
For any non-zero complex number, $z=a+i b(a \neq 0, b \neq 0)$, there exists the complex number $\frac{1}{z}$ or $z^{-1}=\frac{a}{a^{2}+b^{2}}+i \frac{-b}{a^{2}+b^{2}}$ called the multiplicative inverse of $z$ such that $z \cdot \frac{1}{z}=(a+i b)\left(\frac{a}{a^{2}+b^{2}}+i \frac{-b}{a^{2}+b^{2}}\right)=1+i 0=1$
## Solution of a Quadratic Equation
The solutions of quadratic equation $a x^{2}+b x+c=0$, where $a, b, c \in R, a \neq 0$, are given by $x=\frac{-b \pm \sqrt{b^{2}-4 a c}}{2 a}$, where $D=b^{2}-4 a c$ is called the discriminant of the quadratic equation. The value of $D$ may be $+\mathrm{ve},-\mathrm{ve}$ or 0.
When $\mathrm{D}<0$, roots of the quadratic equation are complex (i.e., non-real).
