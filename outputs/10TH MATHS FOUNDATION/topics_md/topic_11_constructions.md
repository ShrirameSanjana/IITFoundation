## 11. Constructions
## DIVISION OF A LINE SEGMENT
In this concept, we will learn the method of dividing a line segment internally in a given ratio.
You can easily understand this concept through the following illustration.
## ILLUSTRATION : 1
Divide a line segment of length 12 cm internally in the ratio $3: 2$.
## SOLUTION :
Steps of construction :
(i) Draw a line segment AB of length 12 cm by using a ruler.
(ii) Draw a ray AX making a suitable acute angle $\angle \mathrm{BAX}$ with AB .
(iii) Along AX , draw $5(=3+2)$ arcs intersecting the ray AX at $\mathrm{A}_{1}, \mathrm{~A}_{2}$, $A_{3}, A_{4}$ and $A_{5}$ such that $A A_{1}=A_{1} A_{2}=A_{2} A_{3}=A_{3} A_{4}=A_{4} A_{5}$
(iv) Join B to $\mathrm{A}_{5}$.
(v) Through $\mathrm{A}_{3}$ draw a line $\mathrm{A}_{3} \mathrm{P}$ parallel to $\mathrm{A}_{5} \mathrm{~B}$ by making $\angle \mathrm{AA}_{3} \mathrm{P}$ equal
to $\angle \mathrm{AA}_{5} \mathrm{~B}$, which intersects AB at point P .
The point P so obtained is the required point, which divides AB internally in the ratio $3: 2$.
## Alternative Method for Division of a Line Segment Internally in a Given Ratio
Use the following steps to divide a given line segment AB internally in a given ratio $m: n$, where $m$ and $n$ are natural numbers.
## Steps of construction :
(i) Draw a line segment AB of given length.
(ii) Draw a ray AX making a suitable acute angle $\angle \mathrm{BAX}$ with AB .
(iii) Draw a ray $B Y$, on opposite side of $A X$ with respect to $A B$, parallel to $A X$ by making an angle $\angle \mathrm{ABY}$ equal to $\angle \mathrm{BAX}$.
(iv) Draw arcs intersecting the ray AX at $A_{1}, A_{2}, \ldots \ldots \ldots ., A_{m}$, and ray BY at $B_{1}$, $B_{2}, \ldots \ldots \ldots ., B_{n}$ such that
$A A_{1}=A_{1} A_{2}=\ldots . .=A_{m-1} A_{m}=B B_{1}=B_{1} B_{2}=\ldots . .=B_{n-1} B_{n}$
(v) Join $\mathrm{A}_{\mathrm{m}} \mathrm{B}_{\mathrm{n}}$. Suppose it intersects AB at P .
The point P is the required point, which divide AB in the ratio $\mathrm{m}: \mathrm{n}$ internally.
## ILLUSTRATION : 2
Divide a line segment of length 6 cm internally in the ratio $3: 4$.
## SOLUTION :
Steps of construction :
(i) Draw a line segment AB of length 6 cm .
(ii) Draw a ray AX making a suitable acute angle $\angle \mathrm{BAX}$ with AB .
(iii) Draw a ray BY parallel to AX by making $\angle \mathrm{ABY}$ equal to $\angle \mathrm{BAX}$.
(iv) Draw three arcs intersecting the ray AX at $A_{1}, A_{2}, A_{3}$ and 4 arcs intersecting the ray BY at $B_{1}, B_{2}, B_{3}, B_{4}$ such that
$A A_{1}=A_{1} A_{2}=A_{2} A_{3}=B B_{1}=B_{1} B_{2}=B_{2} B_{3}=B_{3} B_{4}$.
(v) Join $A_{3} B_{4}$. Suppose it intersects AB at a point P .
Then, P is the point dividing AB internally in the ratio $3: 4$.

## CONSTRUCTION OF A TRIANGLE SIMILAR TO A GIVEN TRIANGLE (AS PER GIVEN SCALE FACTOR)
## Scale Factor
The ratio $m$ : $n$ of the side of the triangle to be constructed and the corresponding side of the given triangle is known as their scale factor.
In this construction, there are two different situations:
(I) Steps of construction when $\boldsymbol{m}<\boldsymbol{n}$ :
(i) Construct the given triangle ABC by using the given data.
(ii) Take any one of the three side of the given triangle as base. Let AB be the base of the given triangle ABC .
(iii) At one end, say A , of the base AB draw a ray AX below AB making a suitable acute angle with AB .
(iv) Along AX mark n points $\mathrm{A}_{1}, \mathrm{~A}_{2}, \mathrm{~A}_{3}, \ldots \ldots \ldots, \mathrm{~A}_{\mathrm{n}}$ such that
$$
\mathrm{AA}_{1}=\mathrm{A}_{1} \mathrm{~A}_{2}=\ldots \ldots \ldots=\mathrm{A}_{\mathrm{n}-1} \mathrm{~A}_{\mathrm{n}} .
$$
(v) Join $\mathrm{A}_{\mathrm{n}}$ to B .
(vi) Draw $A_{m} B^{\prime}$ parallel to $A_{n} B$ by making $\angle \mathrm{A} \mathrm{A}_{\mathrm{m}} \mathrm{B}^{\prime}$ equal to $\angle \mathrm{AA}_{\mathrm{n}} \mathrm{B}$, which meets AB at $\mathrm{B}^{\prime}$.
(vii) From $\mathrm{B}^{\prime}$, draw $\mathrm{B}^{\prime} \mathrm{C}^{\prime}$ parallel to BC by making $\angle \mathrm{AB}^{\prime} \mathrm{C}^{\prime}$ equal to $\angle \mathrm{ABC}$, which meets AC at $\mathrm{C}^{\prime}$. Triangle $\mathrm{AB}^{\prime} \mathrm{C}^{\prime}$ is the required triangle, each of whose sides is $\left(\frac{\mathrm{m}}{\mathrm{n}}\right)^{\text {th }}$ of the corresponding side of $\triangle \mathrm{ABC}$.
## ILLUSTRATION : 3
Construct a $\triangle \mathrm{ABC}$ in which $\mathrm{AB}=5 \mathrm{~cm}, \mathrm{BC}=6 \mathrm{~cm}$ and $\mathrm{AC}=7 \mathrm{~cm}$. Now, construct a triangle similar to $\triangle \mathrm{ABC}$ such that each of its sides is two-third of the corresponding sides of $\triangle \mathrm{ABC}$.
## SOLUTION :
## Steps of construction :
(i) Draw a line segment $\mathrm{AB}=5 \mathrm{~cm}$.
(ii) With A as centre and radius $=7 \mathrm{~cm}$, draw an arc above AB .
(iii) With B as centre and radius $=6 \mathrm{~cm}$, draw another arc, intersecting the arc drawn in step (ii) at C.
(iv) Join A to C and B to C to obtain $\triangle \mathrm{ABC}$.
(v) Below AB , draw a ray AX making a suitable acute angle with AB on opposite side of C with respect to AB .
(vi) Draw three arcs intersecting the ray AX at $\mathrm{A}_{1}, \mathrm{~A}_{2}, \mathrm{~A}_{3}$ such that $\mathrm{AA}_{1}=\mathrm{A}_{1} \mathrm{~A}_{2}= \mathrm{A}_{2} \mathrm{~A}_{3}$.
(vii) Join $\mathrm{A}_{3}$ to B .
(viii) Draw $\mathrm{A}_{3} \mathrm{~B}^{\prime}$ parallel to $\mathrm{A}_{3} \mathrm{~B}^{\prime}$ by making $\angle \mathrm{AA}_{2} \mathrm{~B}^{\prime}$ equal to $\angle \mathrm{AA}_{3} \mathrm{~B}$, which meets AB at $\mathrm{B}^{\prime}$.
(ix) From $\mathrm{B}^{\prime}$, draw $\mathrm{B}^{\prime} \mathrm{C}^{\prime}$ parallel to BC by making $\angle \mathrm{AB}^{\prime} \mathrm{C}^{\prime}$ equal to $\angle \mathrm{ABC}$, which meets AC at $\mathrm{C}^{\prime}$.
$\Delta \mathrm{AB}^{\prime} \mathrm{C}^{\prime}$ is the required triangle, each of the whose sides is two-third of the corresponding sides of $\triangle \mathrm{ABC}$.
(II) Steps of construction when $\boldsymbol{m}>\boldsymbol{n}$ :
(i) Construct the given triangle by using the given data.
(ii) Take any one of the three sides of the given triangle and consider it as the base. Let AB be the base of the given triangle ABC .
(iii) At one end, say A , of base AB draw a ray AX making a suitable acute angle with base AB on the opposite side of the vertex C with respect to AB .
(iv) Draw m arcs intersecting the ray AX at $\mathrm{A}_{1}, \mathrm{~A}_{2}, \mathrm{~A}_{3}, \ldots \ldots \ldots, \mathrm{~A}_{\mathrm{m}}$ such that $\mathrm{AA}_{1}=\mathrm{A}_{1} \mathrm{~A}_{2}=\ldots \ldots \ldots=\mathrm{A}_{\mathrm{m}-1} \mathrm{~A}_{\mathrm{m}}$.
(v) Join $A_{n}$ to $B$
(vi) Draw a line through $\mathrm{A}_{\mathrm{m}}$ parallel to $\mathrm{A}_{\mathrm{n}} \mathrm{B}$ by making $\angle \mathrm{AA}_{\mathrm{m}} \mathrm{B}^{\prime}$ equal to $\angle \mathrm{AA}_{\mathrm{n}} \mathrm{B}$, which intersects the extended line segment AB at $\mathrm{B}^{\prime}$.
(vii) Draw a line through $\mathrm{B}^{\prime}$ parallel to BC by making $\angle \mathrm{AB}^{\prime} \mathrm{C}^{\prime}$ equal to $\angle \mathrm{ABC}$, which intersects the extended line segment AC at $\mathrm{C}^{\prime}$
(viii) $\Delta \mathrm{AB}^{\prime} \mathrm{C}^{\prime}$ so obtained is the required triangle.

Draw a triangle ABC with side $\mathrm{BC}=7 \mathrm{~cm} . \angle \mathrm{B}=45^{\circ}, \angle \mathrm{A}=105^{\circ}$. Construct a triangle whose sides are (4/3) times the corresponding side of $\triangle \mathrm{ABC}$.
## SOLUTION :
## Steps of construction :
(i) Draw $\mathrm{BC}=7 \mathrm{~cm}$.
(ii) Draw a ray BX and CY such that $\angle \mathrm{CBX}=45^{\circ}$ and
$$
\angle \mathrm{BCY}=180^{\circ}-\left(45^{\circ}+105^{\circ}\right)=30^{\circ}
$$
Suppose BX and CY intersect each other at A .
$\triangle \mathrm{ABC}$ so obtained is the given triangle.
(iii) Draw a ray BZ making a suitable acute angle with BC on opposite side of vertex A with respect to BC .
(iv) Draw four arcs intersecting the ray BZ at $\mathrm{B}_{1}, \mathrm{~B}_{2}, \mathrm{~B}_{3}, \mathrm{~B}_{4}$ such that $\mathrm{BB}_{1} =\mathrm{B}_{1} \mathrm{~B}_{2}=\mathrm{B}_{2} \mathrm{~B}_{3}=\mathrm{B}_{3} \mathrm{~B}_{4}$.
(v) Join $\mathrm{B}_{3}$ to C and draw a line through $\mathrm{B}_{4}$ parallel to $\mathrm{B}_{3} \mathrm{C}$ by making $\angle \mathrm{BB}_{4} \mathrm{C}^{\prime}$ equal to $\angle \mathrm{BB}_{3} \mathrm{C}$, which intersects the extended line segment BC at $\mathrm{C}^{\prime}$.
(vi) Draw a line through $\mathrm{C}^{\prime}$ parallel to CA by making $\angle \mathrm{BC}^{\prime} \mathrm{A}^{\prime}$ equal to
$\angle \mathrm{BCA}\left(=30^{\circ}\right)$, which intersects the extended line segment BA at $\mathrm{A}^{\prime}$. Triangle $\mathrm{A}^{\prime} \mathrm{BC}^{\prime}$ so obtained is the required triangle.

## To construct an equilateral triangle circumscribed over a circle of radius $\boldsymbol{r} \mathbf{~ c m}$.
## Steps of Construction
Step 1: Draw a circle of radius $r \mathrm{~cm}$ with centre O .
Step 2: Draw radii OP, OQ and OR such that
$$
\angle \mathrm{POR}=\angle \mathrm{ROQ}=120^{\circ} .\left(\therefore \quad \angle \mathrm{QOP}=120^{\circ}\right)
$$
Step 3: At $\mathrm{P}, \mathrm{Q}$ and R draw perpendiculars to $\mathrm{OP}, \mathrm{OQ}$ and OR respectively to from $\Delta \mathrm{ABC} . \Delta \mathrm{ABC}$ is the required circumscribing equilateral triangle.

Case (a): To draw the tangent to a circle at a given point on it, when the centre of the circle is known :
Required : To draw the tangent to the circle at P .
Steps of construction :
(i) Take a point O as centre and draw a circle of given radius.
(ii) Take a point P on the circle.
(iii) Join OP.
(iv) Draw a line AB perpendicular to OP at the point P . Line APB is the required tangent at P .

not known.
Given : A circle and a point P on it.
Required : To draw the tangent to the circle at P .

(i) Draw any chord PQ through P and join P and Q to a point R in major $\operatorname{arc} \overparen{\mathrm{PQ}}$ (or minor $\operatorname{arc} \overparen{\mathrm{PQ}}$ ).
(ii) Construct $\angle \mathrm{QPB}$ equal to $\angle \mathrm{PRQ}$ and on opposite side of R with respect to chord PQ .
Produce BP to A to get the required tangent APB
## To draw the circum-circle of a given triangle.
## Steps of Construction
Step 1: Draw $\triangle \mathrm{ABC}$ with the given measurements.
Step 2: Draw perpendicular bisectors of the two of the sides, say AB and AC to intersect at O .
Step 3: Taking O as centre, and the radius equal to AO or BO or CO , draw a circle. The circle passes through all the vertices $\mathrm{A}, \mathrm{B}$ and C of the triangle. ∴ The circle drawn is the required circum-circle.
## CONSTRUCTION OF TANGENTS TO A CIRCLE FROM A POINT OUTSIDE THE CIRCLE
In this situation, when the point lies outside the circle, there will be two tangents to the circle from this point.
In this construction, again we have two different situations:
Case (a) : To draw the tangents to a circle from a point outside it (external point), when its centre is known.
Given : A circle with centre O and a point P outside it.
Required : To construct the tangents to the circle from point P .
Steps of construction :
(i) Join OP and bisect it. Let M be the mid-point of OP .
(ii) Taking M as centre and MO as radius, draw a circle which intersect the given circle in two points, say A and B .
(iii) Join P to A and P to $\mathrm{B} . \mathrm{PA}$ and PB are the required tangents from P to given circle .
## ILLUSTRATION : $\mathbf{6}$
Draw a circle of radius 2.5 cm . From a point $\mathrm{P}, 6 \mathrm{~cm}$. apart from the centre of the circle, draw two tangents to the circle.
## SOLUTION :
Given : A point P is at a distance of 6 cm . from the centre of a circle with radius 2.5 cm .
Required : To draw two tangents to the circle from the given point P .
Steps of construction :
(i) Draw a line segment OP of length 6 cm .
(ii) With centre O and radius equal to 2.5 cm , draw a circle.
(iii) Draw the right bisector of OP , which intersects OP at M .
(iv) Taking M as centre and MO as radius, draw a circle, which intersect the given circle in two points, say A and B .
(v) Join PA and PB . These are the required tangents from P to the given circle.
Case (b): To draw the tangents to a circle from a point outside it (external point), when its centre is not known.
Given : A circle and a point P outside it.
Required : To construct the tangents to the circle from point P .
Step of construction :
(i) Through $P$ draw a secant $P A B$, which intersects the circle at points $A$ and $B$ (say).
(ii) Produce $A P$ to a point C such that $A P=P C$ i.e., $P$ is the mid-point of $A C$.
(iii) Draw a semi-circle with $B C$ as diameter.
(iv) Draw $P D \perp C B$, which intersects the semi-circle at $D$.
(v) With $P$ as centre and $P D$ as radius draw arcs to intersect the given circle at $T$ and $T^{\prime}$.
(vi) Join P to T and $\mathrm{T}^{\prime}$. Then PT and $\mathrm{PT}^{\prime}$ are the required tangents.
## ILLUSTRATION : 7
Draw a circle of radius 4 cm . Take a point $P$ outside the circle. Without using the centre of the circle, draw two tangents to the circle from point $\boldsymbol{P}$.
## SOLUTION :
Given : A circle with radius 4 cm and a point P outside it.
Required : To draw two tangents to the circle from the given point P .
## Step of construction :
(i) Draw a circle of radius 4 cm .
(ii) Take a point $P$ outside the circle and draw a secant $P A B$, which intersects the circle at $A$ and $B$.
(iii) Produce $A P$ to $C$ such that $A P=C P$.
(iv) Draw a semi-circle with $C B$ as diameter.
(v) Draw $P D \perp C B$, which intersects the semi-circle at $D$.
(vi) With $P$ as centre and $P D$ as radius draw arcs to intersect the given circle at $T$ and $T^{\prime}$.
(vii) Join $P$ to $T$ and $T^{\prime}$. Then, $P T$ and $P T^{\prime}$ are the required tangents.
To construct the direct common tangents to the circles of radii 3.5 cm and 1.5 cm whose centres are 6.5 cm apart.
Step 1: Draw line segment, $\mathrm{C}_{1} \mathrm{C}_{2}=6.5 \mathrm{~cm}$.
Step 2: Draw circles with radii 3.5 cm and 1.5 cm respectively with $\mathrm{C}_{1}$ and $\mathrm{C}_{2}$ as centres.
Step 3: Bisect $\mathrm{C}_{1} \mathrm{C}_{2}$ at P and draw a circle taking P as centre and radius equal to $\mathrm{PC}_{1}$ or $\mathrm{PC}_{2}$.
Step 4: Draw another circle with radius equal to $(3.5-1.5)=2 \mathrm{~cm}$, taking the centre of larger circle $\mathrm{C}_{1}$ intersecting the circle drawn in step 3 at A and B .
Step 5: Join $\mathrm{C}_{1} \mathrm{~A}$ and $\mathrm{C}_{1} \mathrm{~B}$ and produce $\mathrm{C}_{1} \mathrm{~A}$ and $\mathrm{C}_{1} \mathrm{~B}$ to meet the outer circle at S and T respectively.
Step 6: Draw line segments $C_{2} U$ and $C_{2} V$, such that $C_{2} U \| C_{1} S$ and $C_{2} V \| C_{1} T$, where $U, V$ arc points on the circle with centre $\mathrm{C}_{2}$.
Step 7: Join SU and TV. These are the required direct common tangents.

## MISCELLANEOUS SOLVED EXAMPLES
1. Draw a triangle ABC with side $\mathrm{BC}=8 \mathrm{~cm}, \angle \mathrm{~B}=45^{\circ}, \angle \mathrm{A}=75^{\circ}$. Construct a triangle whose sides are (4/3) times the corresponding side of $\triangle \mathrm{ABC}$.
Sol. Steps of construction :
(i) Draw $\mathrm{BC}=8 \mathrm{~cm}$.
(ii) Draw a ray BX and CY such that $\angle \mathrm{CBX}=45^{\circ}$ and
$$
\angle \mathrm{BCY}=180^{\circ}-\left(45^{\circ}+75^{\circ}\right)=60^{\circ}
$$
Suppose BX and CY intersect at $\mathrm{A} . \triangle \mathrm{ABC}$, so obtained is the given triangle.
(iii) Draw a ray BZ making an acute angle with BC on opposite side of vertex A with respect to BC .
(iv) Draw four (greater of 4 and 3 in 4/3) arcs intersecting the rays BZ at $\mathrm{B}_{1}, \mathrm{~B}_{2}$, $\mathrm{B}_{3}, \mathrm{~B}_{4}$ such that $\mathrm{BB}_{1}=\mathrm{B}_{1} \mathrm{~B}_{2}=\mathrm{B}_{2} \mathrm{~B}_{3}=\mathrm{B}_{3} \mathrm{~B}_{4}$.
(v) Join $\mathrm{B}_{3}$ to C and draw a line through $\mathrm{B}_{4}$ parallel to $\mathrm{B}_{3} \mathrm{C}$, intersecting the extended line segment BC at $\mathrm{C}^{\prime}$.
(vi) Draw a line through $\mathrm{C}^{\prime}$ parallel to CA intersecting the extended line segment BA at $\mathrm{A}^{\prime}$.
Triangle $\mathrm{A}^{\prime} \mathrm{BC}^{\prime}$ so obtained is the required triangle.
2. Construct a $\triangle \mathrm{ABC}$ in which $\mathrm{AB}=6 \mathrm{~cm}, \mathrm{BC}=5 \mathrm{~cm}$ and $\mathrm{AC}=7.5 \mathrm{~cm}$. Now construct a triangle similar to $\triangle \mathrm{ABC}$ such that each of its sides is third-fourth
of the corresponding sides of $\triangle \mathrm{ABC}$. Also prove your assertion.
Sol. Steps of construction :
(i) Draw a line segment $\mathrm{BC}=5 \mathrm{~cm}$.
(ii) With B as centre and radius $=\mathrm{AB}=6 \mathrm{~cm}$, draw an arc.
(iii) With C as centre and radius $=\mathrm{AC}=7.5 \mathrm{~cm}$, draw another arc, intersecting the arc drawn in step (ii) at the point A .
(iv) Join A to B and C to obtain $\triangle \mathrm{ABC}$.
(v) Below BC draw ray BX , making an acute angle $\angle \mathrm{CBX}$.
(vi) Draw four arcs intersecting the ray BX at $\mathrm{B}_{1}, \mathrm{~B}_{2}, \mathrm{~B}_{3}, \mathrm{~B}_{4}$ such that $\mathrm{BB}_{1}=\mathrm{B}_{1} \mathrm{~B}_{2}=\mathrm{B}_{2} \mathrm{~B}_{3}=\mathrm{B}_{3} \mathrm{~B}_{4}$
(vii) Join $\mathrm{B}_{4} \mathrm{C}$.
(viii) Since we have to construct a triangle each of whose sides is third-fourth of the corresponding sides of $\triangle \mathrm{ABC}$. Hence draw $\mathrm{B}_{3} \mathrm{C}^{\prime} \| \mathrm{B}_{4} \mathrm{C}$.
(ix) From $\mathrm{C}^{\prime}$, draw $\mathrm{C}^{\prime} \mathrm{A}^{\prime} \| \mathrm{CA}$, intersecting BA at point $\mathrm{A}^{\prime}$. Then $\triangle \mathrm{A}^{\prime} \mathrm{BC}^{\prime}$ is the required triangle.
3. Construct a triangle similar to a given equilateral $\triangle \mathrm{PQR}$ with side 5 cm such that each
of its sides is $\frac{\mathbf{6}}{\mathbf{7}}$ th of the corresponding side of $\triangle \mathrm{PQR}$.
Sol. Steps of construction :
(i) Draw a line segment $\mathrm{QR}=5 \mathrm{~cm}$
(ii) With Q as centre and radius $=5 \mathrm{~cm}$, draw an arc.
(iii) With R as centre and radius $=5 \mathrm{~cm}$, draw another arc intersecting the arc drawn in step (ii) at the point P .
(iv) Join PQ and PR to obtain $\triangle \mathrm{PQR}$.
(v) Below QR , draw ray QX making an acute angle $\angle \mathrm{RQX}$.
(vi) Draw seven arcs intersecting the ray QX at $\mathrm{Q}_{1}, \mathrm{Q}_{2}, \ldots . \mathrm{Q}_{7}$; such that
$\mathrm{QQ}_{1}=\mathrm{Q}_{1} \mathrm{Q}_{2}=\mathrm{Q}_{2} \mathrm{Q}_{3}=\ldots . .=\mathrm{Q}_{6} \mathrm{Q}_{7}$
(vii) Join $\mathrm{Q}_{7} \mathrm{R}$.
(viii) Draw $\mathrm{Q}_{6} \mathrm{R}^{\prime} \| \mathrm{Q}_{7} \mathrm{R}$, intersecting QR at $\mathrm{R}^{\prime}$
(ix) From $\mathrm{R}^{\prime}$ draw $\mathrm{R}^{\prime} \mathrm{P}^{\prime} \| \mathrm{RP}$, intersecting the QP at $\mathrm{P}^{\prime}$.
We get required $\triangle P Q R^{\prime}$ similar to $\triangle P Q R$.
4. Construct a triangle similar to a given triangle ABC with its sides equal to $\frac{\mathbf{7}}{\mathbf{5}}$ th of the corresponding sides of triangle ABC .
Sol. Steps of construction :
(i) Draw a ray BX making a suitable acute angle with BC on opposite side of A.
(ii) Draw seven arcs intersecting the ray BX at $\mathrm{X}_{1}, \mathrm{X}_{2}, \ldots \ldots, \mathrm{X}_{7}$ such that $\mathrm{BX}_{1}=\mathrm{X}_{1} \mathrm{X}_{2}=\mathrm{X}_{2} \mathrm{X}_{3}=\mathrm{X}_{3} \mathrm{X}_{4}=\mathrm{X}_{4} \mathrm{X}_{5}=\mathrm{X}_{5} \mathrm{X}_{6}=\mathrm{X}_{6} \mathrm{X}_{7}$
(iii) Join $\mathrm{X}_{5}$ to C and draw a line $\mathrm{X}_{7} \mathrm{C}^{\prime}$ parallel to $\mathrm{X}_{5} \mathrm{C}$, which intersects BC producted at $\mathrm{C}^{\prime}$.
(iv) Draw a line $\mathrm{C}^{\prime} \mathrm{A}^{\prime}$ parallel to CA to intersect BA producted at $\mathrm{A}^{\prime}$. Then $\Delta \mathrm{A}^{\prime} \mathrm{BC}^{\prime}$ will be the required triangle.
5. Construct a triangle ABC whose sides are $7.5 \mathrm{~cm}, 7 \mathrm{~cm}$ and 6.5 cm . Construct a triangle similar to $\triangle \mathrm{ABC}$ with sides $\frac{\mathbf{2}}{\mathbf{3}} \mathbf{r d}$ of the corresponding sides of triangle $\triangle \mathbf{A B C}$.
Sol. Steps of construction :

(ii) With C as centre and radius 6.5 cm , draw arc intersecting the arc drawn in step (i) at point A . Then $\triangle \mathrm{ABC}$ is the required triangle.
(iii) Draw a ray BX making an acute angle with BC opposite side of A with respect to BC .
(iv) Draw three arcs intersecting the ray BX at $\mathrm{X}_{1}, \mathrm{X}_{2}$ and $\mathrm{X}_{3}$ such that $\mathrm{BX}_{1}=\mathrm{X}_{1} \mathrm{X}_{2}= \mathrm{X}_{2} \mathrm{X}_{3}$.
(v) Join $\mathrm{X}_{3} \mathrm{C}$.
(vi) Draw $\mathrm{X}_{2} \mathrm{C}^{\prime} \| \mathrm{X}_{3} \mathrm{C}$, which intersects BC at $\mathrm{C}^{\prime}$.
(vii) Draw $\mathrm{C}^{\prime} \mathrm{A}^{\prime} \| \mathrm{CA}$, which intersects BA at $\mathrm{A}^{\prime}$. Then $\triangle \mathrm{A}^{\prime} \mathrm{BC}^{\prime}$ is the required triangle.
6. Let $\triangle \mathrm{ABC}$ be a right triangle in which $\mathrm{AB}=3 \mathrm{~cm}, \mathrm{BC}=4 \mathrm{~cm}$ and $\angle \mathrm{B}=\mathbf{9 0}^{\circ}$. BD is the perpendicular from B to AC . The circle through $B, C, D$ is drawn. Construct the tangents from $A$ to this circle.
Sol. Steps of construction :
(i) Draw line segments $\mathrm{BC}=4 \mathrm{~cm}$ and $\mathrm{AB}=3 \mathrm{~cm}$ perpendicular to BC . Join $\mathrm{AC} . \triangle \mathrm{ABC}$ is the right triangle.
(ii) Draw $\mathrm{BD} \perp \mathrm{AC}$
(iii) Draw perpendicular bisector of BC , which intersects BC at E .
(iv) Draw a circle with centre E and radius EB (or EC ) clearly if passes through the point $\mathrm{B}, \mathrm{C}$ and D . Now AB is the tangent to the circle drawn at B
(v) With centre A and radius equal to AB , draw an arc intersecting the circle at F (other than the point B ).
(vi) Draw ray AF .
Thus AB and AF are two required tangents.
