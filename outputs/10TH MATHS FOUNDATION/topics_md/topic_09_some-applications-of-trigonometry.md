## ANGLE OF ELEVATION AND ANGLE OF DEPRESSION
Let an observer at the point O is observing an object at the point P . The line OP is called the LINE OF SIGHT of the point P . Let OA be the horizontal line in the vertical plane passing through OP . If object P be above the horizontal line OA , then the acute angle AOP, between the line of sight and the horizontal line is known as ANGLE OF ELEVATION of object P. If the object P is below the horizontal line OA , then the acute angle AOP , between the line of sight and horizontal line is known as $\mathbf{A N G L E}$ OF DEPRESSION of object P.

Fig. (ii)
In fig. (i), $\angle \mathrm{AOP}=$ Angle of elevation.
In fig. (ii), $\angle \mathrm{AOP}=$ Angle of depression.

## TO FIND THE HEIGHT AND THE DISTANCE OF TOWER STANDING ON A HORIZONTAL PLANE
Let AB be a tower and B be its foot. On the horizontal line through B , take two points P and Q . Measure the length PQ .
Let $\mathrm{PQ}=a$.
Let the angles of elevation of the top A of the tower as seen from P and Q be respectively $\alpha$ and $\beta(\beta>\alpha)$, then $\angle \mathrm{APB}=\alpha$, $\angle \mathrm{AQB}=\beta$.
Let $A B=x, B Q=y$.
From right angled $\triangle \mathrm{ABP}, \tan \alpha=\frac{A B}{P B}=\frac{x}{a+y}$
$\therefore a+y=x \cot \alpha$
From right angled $\triangle A B Q, \tan \beta=\frac{A B}{B Q}=\frac{x}{y}$
$\therefore y=x \cot \beta$
From equations (i) and (ii),
$\therefore a=x \cot \alpha-x \cot \beta \Rightarrow x=\frac{a}{\cot \alpha-\cot \beta}$
Also, $y=x \cot \alpha-a$
$\Rightarrow y=\frac{a \cot \alpha}{\cot \alpha-\cot \beta}-a \Rightarrow y=\frac{a \cot \alpha-a(\cot \alpha-\cot \beta)}{\cot \alpha-\cot \beta} \Rightarrow y=\frac{a \cot \beta}{\cot \alpha-\cot \beta}$
In the above case, $P$ and $Q$ are on the same side of the tower.
If the two points are on the opposite sides of the tower then from the adjoining figure, we get
$$
\begin{aligned}
& \tan \alpha=\frac{x}{P B} \text { or } P B=x \cot \alpha \text { and } \tan \beta=\frac{x}{B Q} \text { or } B Q=x \cot \beta . \\
& \therefore a=P B+B Q=x(\cot \alpha+\cot \beta), \therefore x=\frac{a}{\cot \alpha+\cot \beta} \text { and } y=B Q=x \cot \beta
\end{aligned}
$$
Note : Here, all the lines $A P, A Q, A B$ are in the same plane.
The angle of elevation of a cliff from a fixed point is $\theta$. After going up a distance of $\boldsymbol{k}$ meters towards the top of the cliff at an angle of $\phi$, it is found that the angle of elevation is $\alpha$. Show that the height of the cliff is
$$
\frac{k(\cos \phi-\sin \phi \cot \alpha)}{\cot \theta-\cot \alpha} \text { meters }
$$
## SOLUTION :
$\mathrm{MP}=h$ meters be the height of the cliff
$\angle \mathrm{MAP}=\theta$ is the angle of elevation of the top P as observed from the fixed point A .
$\angle \mathrm{PCD}=\alpha$, where $\mathrm{AC}=k$ meters and $\angle \mathrm{CAB}=\phi$
Draw $\quad \mathrm{CD} \perp \mathrm{MP}$ and $\mathrm{CB} \perp \mathrm{AM}$
P(Top of
Here, $\quad \mathrm{CD}=\mathrm{BM}$ and $\mathrm{BC}=\mathrm{MD}$.
From, rt. $\triangle \mathrm{ABC}$, we have
$\frac{B C}{A C}=\sin \phi$ and $\frac{A B}{A C}=\cos \phi \Rightarrow \frac{B C}{k}=\sin \phi$ and $\frac{A B}{k}=\cos \phi$
$\Rightarrow \quad B C=k \sin \phi$ and $A B=k \cos \phi$
From, rt. $\triangle \mathrm{AMP}$,
$$
\frac{M P}{A M}=\tan \theta \Rightarrow \frac{h}{A M}=\tan \theta \Rightarrow A M=h \cot \theta
$$
From, rt. $\triangle \mathrm{CDP}$,
$$
\frac{D P}{C D}=\tan \alpha \Rightarrow \tan \alpha=\frac{M P-M D}{B M}=\frac{M P-B C}{A M-A B}
$$
$\Rightarrow \quad \tan \alpha=\frac{h-k \sin \phi}{h \cot \theta-k \cos \phi} \quad$ (From (1), (2) and (3))
$\Rightarrow \quad \frac{1}{\cot \alpha}=\frac{h-k \sin \phi}{h \cot \theta-k \cos \phi}$
$\Rightarrow \quad h=\frac{k(\cos \phi-\cot \alpha \sin \phi)}{\cot \theta-\cot \alpha} \Rightarrow h=\frac{k(\cos \phi-\sin \phi \cot \alpha)}{\cot \theta-\cot \alpha}$.
## CONNECTING TOPIC
Bearing : If the observer and the object are on same plane (level), then bearing is defined. A bearing is an angle , measured clockwise or anticlockwise from the any one of four standard direction East, West, North and South.
## ILLUSTRATION : 1
If the bearing from $A$ to $B$ is $E 30^{\circ} \mathrm{S}$, then find the bearing from $B$ to $A$ from North to West direction.
## SOLUTION :
Since, the bearing from A to B is $\mathrm{E} 30^{\circ} \mathrm{S}$ means $30^{\circ}$ from East to South.
$\therefore \angle \mathrm{EAB}=30^{\circ}$
Now, we have to find the bearing from B to A from North to West direction.
i.e. $\angle \mathrm{ABN}=$ ?
Let $\angle \mathrm{ABN}=x$.
$\angle \mathrm{EAB}=\angle \mathrm{ABW}=30^{\circ}$ [Alternate interior angles]
$\because \angle \mathrm{NBA}+\angle \mathrm{ABW}=90^{\circ}$
$\therefore x+30^{\circ}=90^{\circ}$
$\square \Rightarrow x=90^{\circ}-30^{\circ}=60^{\circ}$
Thus, the bearing from B to A from North to West direction is $60^{\circ}$.

Two stations due south of a leaning tower which leans towards the north are at distances $\boldsymbol{a}$ metres and $\boldsymbol{b}$ metres from its foot. If $\alpha, \beta$ be the elevations of the top of the tower from these stations, prove that inclination $\theta$ of the leaning tower to the horizontal is given by $\cot \theta=\frac{b \cot \alpha-a \cot \beta}{b-a}$.
## SOLUTION :
MP is the tower which is at a inclination $\theta$ to the horizontal.
Let NP $=h$ meters be the vertical height of the top of the tower from the level ground.
A and B are two stations such that $\mathrm{AM}=a$ metres and $\mathrm{BM}=b$ metres
Let $\mathrm{MN}=x$ metres.
In rt. $\triangle \mathrm{MNP}$,
$$
\begin{aligned}
& \frac{P N}{M N}=\tan \theta \Rightarrow \frac{h}{x}=\tan \theta \\
\Rightarrow & x=\frac{h}{\tan \theta} \Rightarrow x=h \cot \theta
\end{aligned}
$$
In rt. $\triangle \mathrm{ANP}$,
$$
\begin{aligned}
& \frac{P N}{A N}=\tan \alpha \Rightarrow \frac{h}{x+a}=\tan \alpha \\
\Rightarrow & x+a=h \cot \alpha \Rightarrow x=h \cot \alpha-a
\end{aligned}
$$
In rt. $\triangle B N P$,
$$
\begin{aligned}
& \frac{P N}{B N}=\tan \beta \Rightarrow \frac{h}{b+x}=\tan \beta \\
\Rightarrow & b+x=\frac{h}{\tan \beta} \Rightarrow x=h \cot \beta-b
\end{aligned}
$$
From (1) and (2),
$$
\begin{aligned}
\quad h \cot \theta=h \cot \alpha-a & \Rightarrow a=h \cot \alpha-h \cot \theta \\
\Rightarrow \quad h(\cot \alpha-\cot \theta)=a & \Rightarrow h=\frac{a}{(\cot \alpha-\cot \theta)}
\end{aligned}
$$
From (1) and (3), we get
$h \cot \theta=h \cot \beta-b \Rightarrow b=h \cot \beta-h \cot \theta$
$\Rightarrow h=\frac{b}{\cot \beta-\cot \theta}$
From (4) and (5), we get
$$
\frac{a}{\cot \alpha-\cot \theta}=\frac{b}{\cot \beta-\cot \theta} \Rightarrow a(\cot \beta-\cot \theta)=b(\cot \alpha-\cot \theta) \Rightarrow \cot \theta=\frac{b \cot \alpha-a \cot \beta}{b-a}
$$

## MISCELLANEOUS SOLVED EXAMPLES
1. The angles of elevation of the top of a tower from the top and the foot of a pole of height $\mathbf{1 0 ~ m}$ are $\mathbf{3 0}^{\circ}$ and $\mathbf{6 0}^{\circ}$ respectively. Find the height of the tower.
Sol. Let AB and CD be the pole and tower respectively.
Let $C D=h \mathrm{~m}$
Then, $\angle \mathrm{DAC}=60^{\circ}$ and $\angle \mathrm{DBE}=30^{\circ}$
Now, $\frac{C D}{C A}=\tan 60^{\circ}=\sqrt{3}, \therefore C D=\sqrt{3} C A \Rightarrow \frac{h}{\sqrt{3}}=C A$
Again, $\frac{D E}{B E}=\tan 30^{\circ}=\frac{1}{\sqrt{3}}$
$\therefore(h-10)=\frac{B E}{\sqrt{3}}=\frac{C A}{\sqrt{3}}=\frac{h / \sqrt{3}}{\sqrt{3}}=\frac{h}{3} \quad[\because B E=C A]$
$\Rightarrow 3 h-30=h \Rightarrow 2 h=30 \Rightarrow h=15$.
Hence, height of the tower $=15 \mathrm{~m}$.
2. A man is standing on the deck of a ship, which is $\mathbf{8 ~ m}$ above water level. He observes the angle of elevation of the top of a hill as $60^{\circ}$ and the angle of depression of the base of the hill as $30^{\circ}$. Calculate the distance of the hill from the ship and the height of the hill.
Sol. Let $x m$ be the distance of hill from man and $(h+8) m$ be the height of hill which is required.
In rt. $\triangle A C B$,
$\tan 60^{\circ}=\frac{A C}{B C}=\frac{h}{x} \Rightarrow \sqrt{3}=\frac{h}{x} \Rightarrow h=\sqrt{3} x[\because \mathrm{ED}=\mathrm{BC}=x]$
In rt. $\triangle B C D$,
$\tan 30^{\circ}=\frac{C D}{B C}=\frac{8}{x} \Rightarrow \frac{1}{\sqrt{3}}=\frac{8}{x} \Rightarrow x=8 \sqrt{3}$
Distance of ship from hill $=x=8 \sqrt{3} \mathrm{~m}$
∴ Height of hill $=h+8=\sqrt{3} x+8=(\sqrt{3})(8 \sqrt{3})+8=32 \mathrm{~m}$.
3. A vertical tower stands on a horizontal plane and is surmounted by a vertical flag staff of height 5 meters. At point on the plane, the angle of elevation of the bottom and the top of the flag staff are respectively $\mathbf{3 0}^{\boldsymbol{\circ}}$ and $\mathbf{6 0}^{\boldsymbol{\circ}}$. Find the height of the tower.
Sol. Let $A B$ be the tower of height $h$ meter and $B C$ be the height of flag staff surmounted on the tower.
Let the point of the plane be $D$ at a distance $x$ meter from the foot of the tower.
In rt. $\triangle A B D$,
$\tan 30^{\circ}=\frac{A B}{A D} \Rightarrow \frac{1}{\sqrt{3}}=\frac{h}{x} \Rightarrow x=\sqrt{3} h$
In rt. $\triangle \mathrm{ADC}$,
$\tan 60^{\circ}=\frac{A C}{A D} \Rightarrow \sqrt{3}=\frac{5+h}{x} \Rightarrow x=\frac{5+h}{\sqrt{3}}$
From (i) and (ii), $\sqrt{3} h=\frac{5+h}{\sqrt{3}} \Rightarrow 3 h=5+h \Rightarrow 2 h=5 \Rightarrow h=\frac{5}{2}=2.5 \mathrm{~m}$.
So, the height of tower $=2.5 \mathrm{~m}$.
4. The angle of elevation of an aeroplane from a point on the ground is $45^{\circ}$. After a flight of 15 sec , the elevation changes to $\mathbf{3 0}^{\circ}$. If the aeroplane is flying at a height of $\mathbf{3 0 0 0}$ metres, find the speed of the aeroplane.
Sol. Let the point on the ground is E which is y metres from point B and let after 15 sec ., flight covers $x$ metres distance
In rt. $\triangle A E B, \tan 45^{\circ}=\frac{A B}{E B} \Rightarrow 1=\frac{3000}{y} \Rightarrow y=3000 \mathrm{~m}$
In rt. $\triangle C E D, \tan 30^{\circ}=\frac{C D}{E D} \Rightarrow \frac{1}{\sqrt{3}}=\frac{3000}{x+y} \quad(\because A B=C D)$
$\Rightarrow x+y=3000 \sqrt{3}$
From eqs. (i) and (ii)
$$
\begin{aligned}
& x+3000=3000 \sqrt{3} \Rightarrow x=3000(\sqrt{3}-1) \Rightarrow x=3000 \times(1.732-1) \\
\Rightarrow & x=3000 \times 0.732 \Rightarrow x=2196 \mathrm{~m}
\end{aligned}
$$
Speed of aeroplane $=\frac{\text { Distance covered }}{\text { Time taken }}$
$=\frac{2196}{15} \mathrm{~m} / \mathrm{sec}=146.4 \mathrm{~m} / \mathrm{sec}=\frac{2196}{15} \times \frac{18}{5} \mathrm{~km} / \mathrm{hr}=527.04 \mathrm{~km} / \mathrm{hr}$.
Hence, the speed of aeroplane is $527.04 \mathrm{~km} / \mathrm{hr}$.
5. There is a small island in the middle of a 100 m wide river and a tall tree stands on the island. $P$ and $Q$ are points directly opposite to each other on the two banks, and in line with the tree. If the angles of elevation of the top of the tree from $P$ and $Q$ are respectively $30^{\circ}$ and $45^{\circ}$, find the height of the tree.
Sol. Let $T A$ be the tree.
Let the height of the tree be $h$ metres, i.e., $\quad \mathrm{AT}=h \mathrm{~m}$
In rt. $\triangle \mathrm{QAT}, \tan 45^{\circ}=\frac{h}{x} \Rightarrow h=x\left(\because \tan 45^{\circ}=1\right)$
In rt. $\triangle \mathrm{PAT}, \tan 30^{\circ}=\frac{h}{100-x}$
$\Rightarrow \frac{1}{\sqrt{3}}=\frac{h}{100-x}[\because x=h]$
$\Rightarrow \sqrt{3} h=100-h \Rightarrow(\sqrt{3}+1) h=100$
$\Rightarrow \quad h=\frac{100}{\sqrt{3}+1} \times \frac{\sqrt{3}-1}{\sqrt{3}-1}=50(\sqrt{3}-1)=36.6 \mathrm{~m}$.
∴ The height of the tree is 36.6 m

7. The shadow of a tower, when the angle of elevation of the sun is $\mathbf{4 5}^{\circ}$, is found to be $\mathbf{1 0}$ metres longer than when it is $60^{\circ}$. Find the height of the tower.
Sol. Let length of tower $A B=x \mathrm{~m}$.
Angle of elevation at point $C=60^{\circ}$. Shadow of tower, $B C=y \mathrm{~m}, C D=10 \mathrm{~m}$
Now in rt. $\triangle A B C, \frac{A B}{B C}=\tan 60^{\circ} \Rightarrow \frac{x}{y}=\sqrt{3} \Rightarrow y=\frac{x}{\sqrt{3}}$
In rt. $\triangle A B D, \frac{A B}{B D}=\tan 45^{\circ} \Rightarrow \frac{x}{10+y}=1 \Rightarrow x=10+y$
Putting the value of $y$ in (ii), we get
$$
\begin{aligned}
& x=10+\frac{x}{\sqrt{3}} \Rightarrow \sqrt{3} x=10 \sqrt{3}+x \\
\Rightarrow & x(\sqrt{3}-1)=10 \sqrt{3} \Rightarrow x=\frac{10 \sqrt{3}}{\sqrt{3}-1} \times \frac{\sqrt{3}+1}{\sqrt{3}+1} \Rightarrow x=\frac{10 \sqrt{3}(\sqrt{3}+1)}{2} \\
\Rightarrow & x=5 \sqrt{3}(\sqrt{3}+1) \Rightarrow x=15+5 \sqrt{3} \\
\Rightarrow & x=15+5(1.73) \Rightarrow x=15+8.65=23.65 \mathrm{~m}
\end{aligned}
$$
8. A person observed the angle of elevation of the top of a tower as $30^{\circ}$. He walked 50 m towards the foot of the tower along level ground and found the angle of elevation of the top of the tower as $\mathbf{6 0}^{\circ}$. Find the height of the tower.
Sol. Suppose, height of the tower $A B=x m$ and distance $B C=y \mathrm{~m}$.
In rt. $\triangle A B C, \frac{A B}{B C}=\tan 60^{\circ} \Rightarrow \frac{x}{y}=\sqrt{3} \Rightarrow y=\frac{x}{\sqrt{3}}$
In rt. $\triangle A B D, \frac{A B}{D B}=\tan 30^{\circ} \Rightarrow \frac{x}{50+y}=\frac{1}{\sqrt{3}} \Rightarrow \sqrt{3} x=50+y$
Putting the value of $y$ in (ii), we get, $\sqrt{3} x=50+\frac{x}{\sqrt{3}}$
$\Rightarrow \sqrt{3} x \times \sqrt{3}=50 \sqrt{3}+x \Rightarrow 3 x=50 \sqrt{3}+x \Rightarrow x=25 \sqrt{3}$.
Hence, height of the tower $=25 \sqrt{3} \mathrm{~m}$.
